Indian Taxation Quick Guide

Understand how tax laws and optimization brackets apply to you.

The New Regime (default since FY 2023-24) offers lower slab rates but removes almost all deductions (like 80C, 80D, HRA). The Old Regime has higher tax slabs but allows you to deduct tax-saving investments (up to ₹1.5L in 80C, home loan interest, HRA, etc.) to lower your net taxable income.
Under the revised New Tax Regime in Budget 2025:
  • • Up to ₹4,00,000: Nil
  • • ₹4,00,001 to ₹8,00,000: 5%
  • • ₹8,00,001 to ₹12,00,000: 10%
  • • ₹12,00,001 to ₹16,00,000: 15%
  • • ₹16,00,001 to ₹20,00,000: 20%
  • • ₹20,00,001 to ₹24,00,000: 25%
  • • Above ₹24,00,000: 30%
Additionally, standard deduction is increased to ₹75,000 and the Section 87A rebate limit is raised to ₹12 Lakhs.
Presumptive taxation allows businesses (turnover < ₹3Cr) or specified professionals (receipts < ₹75L) to declare tax on a flat presumptive rate (8%/6% for business, 50% for professionals) without maintaining account books.
